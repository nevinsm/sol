## Approach

This is the **first pass** — your job is to produce a complete working draft. Breadth over depth. Cover every requirement, don't polish.

### Before You Start

1. **Read existing files.** If you are modifying existing files, read them thoroughly before making any changes. Understand what's already there.
2. **Identify all deliverables.** Re-read the assignment above and list every concrete thing that needs to exist when you're done.
3. **Plan your order of work.** Tackle the highest-risk or most uncertain pieces first.

### While You Work

- **Completeness is the priority.** A rough implementation of every requirement beats a polished implementation of half of them. You will not get another chance to add missing pieces — later passes only refine what exists.
- **For code:** Get the logic right at a structural level. Write all the functions, handle the main paths, create the files. Don't worry about perfect naming, exhaustive error handling, or edge cases — those come in later passes.
- **For writing:** Get all sections drafted. Cover every topic. Don't agonize over word choice or flow — just get the ideas down in a logical order.
- **For configuration:** Create all required files with all required fields. Use reasonable values. Don't worry about optimization or perfect formatting.
- **Make it work, not beautiful.** If you find yourself spending time on style, naming, or micro-optimization — stop. Move on to the next requirement.

### When You're Done

- Verify you have addressed every deliverable from the assignment.
- Commit your work with a clear message describing what you drafted.
- Do not attempt to refine or polish — that is explicitly handled by subsequent passes.
